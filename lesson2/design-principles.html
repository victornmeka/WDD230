Alignment

Ryan's Cleaning Service

In design principles, alignment refers to the way the different elements in a design are organised or arranged in relation to each other or to part of the pages like the borders or edges. The elements can be centered, right-aligned or left-aligned relative to other items in the design.
In this example of a mobile facebook login webpage, we see several items aligned centrally to the webpage, the heading and logo(facebook), the the login button, the forotten password link and also the create account button. There are also enough white space between each elements.

Constrast

Piggyvest Saving Platform

Constrast is the use of visible difference to attract attention. It basically determines the first thing you want a viewer to look at. It is designing a distinct difference between the foreground and background colors in order to enhance readability and user interaction.
In this example, we see the black "create free account" button having a background quite different from the webpage white background color. The constrast exists to persuade users to click on them so they begin interacting with the page immediately.

Visual Hierachy

Samsung

Visual Hierachy is the principle of systematically arranging webpage elements according to the order of their importance.
Visual Hierachy is used to rank elements in the order you want your users to view them.
In this example, we first see the brand logo followed by the link to explore their products(phones).
We also see a bold hero image advertising their latest products containing the "Reserve now" link to explore their newest product and then the image of the product all systematically arranged.
